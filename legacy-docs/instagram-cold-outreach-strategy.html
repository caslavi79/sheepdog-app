How to Find Targets

Start with the named businesses in your target lists. Search each one on Instagram. If they have an account, that's a DM target. If they don't, look for the owner or manager's personal account. Beyond the lists, here's how to find more.

Events

  • Search location tags: Bryan TX, College Station TX, Northgate, Kyle Field, Texas A&M
  • Look through followers of venues in your target list -- The Stella Hotel, Messina Hof, Peach Creek Ranch, etc.
  • Search hashtags: #bcswedding, #aggiewed, #collegestationevents, #northgatebcs, #brazoswedding, #tamuformal
  • Check who's posting from local bars on weekends
  • Follow TAMU Greek life accounts and look at who's organizing events
  • Follow @aroundbcs, @outandaboutbcs, and @thebeecommunitybcs -- engage with their content to build local visibility

Staffing

  • Search for companies in your Staffing target list on Instagram and LinkedIn
  • Look through Bryan-College Station Chamber of Commerce followers (@bcschamber)
  • Search hashtags: #bryantx, #collegestationtx, #bcsjobs, #brazosjobs, #hiringtexas
  • Find local construction companies posting project updates or "now hiring" content
  • Property management companies are most active May-August (turnover season) -- time outreach for spring

Follow-Up Sequence

Day 3 (no reply)

Just bumping this up. No rush at all, just wanted to make sure it didn't get buried. Let me know if you'd ever want to chat.

Day 7 (still no reply)

Hey, totally get it if the timing isn't right. If you ever need [security/bartending/staffing] for something down the road, we're here. Feel free to save my info.

Day 14 (final)

Last one from me. Not trying to blow up your inbox. If anything comes up where you need [the service], we're a quick call away. Good luck with everything.

Stop after the third follow-up. Don't message again unless they reply or engage with your content later.

Outreach Cadence

Daily volume10-15 new DMs per day per account. Don't go over 20, Instagram will flag you.
Best timesTuesday through Thursday, 9am-11am or 7pm-9pm. Don't send on weekends unless it's a reply.
SpacingSpread DMs across the day. Don't send 15 in 5 minutes.
TrackingSimple spreadsheet: name, account, date sent, replied y/n, follow-up date, notes.

Rules

Do

  • Personalize the first line. Mention their venue, company, or something they posted.
  • Keep it short. If it doesn't fit on one phone screen, it's too long.
  • Use their name if you can find it.
  • Be casual. You're a local business owner, not a sales robot.
  • Engage with their content before you DM. Like a post or two, leave a real comment. DM the next day.

Don't

  • Copy paste the exact same message to everyone. Instagram can tell.
  • Use words like "opportunity", "partnership", or "synergy".
  • Send a link in the first message. That's spam behavior.
  • DM from a new account with no posts. Have at least 9-12 posts before outreach.
  • Follow up more than 3 times. If they don't reply, move on.
  • DM competitors' clients trying to poach. Small town, word gets around.
