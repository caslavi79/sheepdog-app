01 - Brand Positioning
What Sheepdog Events Is

One paragraph. What it is, who it serves, why it's different. Every piece of Events marketing traces back to this.

Sheepdog Events is the event security and mobile bartending arm of Sheepdog, providing experienced, vetted personnel for bars, weddings, concerts, festivals, private parties, and Greek life events across the Bryan–College Station area. We serve venue owners, event planners, and individuals who need their event to run smoothly and safely - without the security feeling heavy-handed or the bar service feeling like an afterthought. What makes us different: we handle both security and bartending under one roof, so clients deal with one team, one point of contact, and one invoice. Our people show up trained, uniformed, and ready - because the best security is the kind your guests barely notice, and the best bartending is the kind they remember.

i

Use this positioning statement as the foundation for all Events marketing copy, website headers, pitch decks, and outreach. Every piece of content should be traceable back to this paragraph. If copy drifts from these themes, it's off-brand.

02 - Brand Voice
How Sheepdog Events Sounds

Four attributes. Every piece of copy - website, DM, email, social post - should sound like all four of these.

Dependable
We do what we say. No surprises, no no-shows. The client can stop worrying the moment they book us.
✓ Do

"We'll be there at 8. Uniformed, briefed, and ready before your first guest arrives."

✗ Don't

"We'll try to get the team there on time depending on availability."

Experienced
We've worked the door, managed the crowd, poured the drinks. This isn't our first event - it's our hundredth.
✓ Do

"Our team has worked everything from 50-person weddings to 2,000-capacity festivals."

✗ Don't

"We are a new and exciting company eager to gain experience in event security."

Straightforward
No jargon, no runaround. We say what it costs, what's included, and what to expect.
✓ Do

"3 guards, 5 hours, $30/hr each. That's $450. We'll send a quote in 10 minutes."

✗ Don't

"Our bespoke security solutions are tailored to your unique event requirements. Contact us for a customized proposal."

Warm
We're security, not soldiers. We're bartenders, not robots. Approachable, friendly, human. We protect the vibe, not just the door.
✓ Do

"Your guests should feel safe and have a great time. That's the whole point."

✗ Don't

"Our trained operatives will secure the perimeter and enforce access control protocols."

03 - Messaging Pillars
Three Themes We Always Return To

Every piece of Sheepdog Events marketing ties back to at least one of these.

01

Security Without the Scene

The best security is the kind your guests don't think about. We keep things safe without making it feel like a checkpoint. Our team blends in - visible enough to deter, subtle enough to not disrupt. The party keeps going. We handle the rest.

02

One Team, One Call

Security and bartending under one roof. No coordinating two vendors, two invoices, two schedules. One point of contact handles everything - from the door to the bar. This is the biggest differentiator and should be in every pitch.

03

We Show Up Ready

Trained, uniformed, on time, every time. No scrambling, no last-minute fills, no excuses. When you book Sheepdog Events, the team that shows up has been briefed on your venue, your event, and your expectations. Dependability is the product.

05 - When & Rules
Applying the Dual-Mode System

Context decides which mode. The event type and audience determine which color leads.

Use Security Mode When

  • Bar / nightclub security materials
  • Concert and festival proposals
  • Fight night, boxing, high-energy crowds
  • Door staff and crowd management promos
  • The sheepdogtexas.com/events hero
  • Combined security + bartending pitches
  • Anything where "authority" leads

Use Bartending Mode When

  • Wedding bar service proposals
  • Cocktail hour and reception marketing
  • Private party bartending promos
  • Corporate event bar service
  • Menu cards, drink lists, bar signage
  • Instagram posts about bartending work
  • Anything where "warmth" leads
i

Combined events: Default to Security Mode for the pitch - authority sells trust. Once booked, bartending-specific materials (menu cards, bar setup) can flip to Bartending Mode.

Amber Is the Anchor

Amber #C9922E appears in both modes as the primary accent. It's on every CTA button (always with black text - 7.3:1 AAA), every accent. If amber is missing, it doesn't look like Sheepdog Events.

Never Mix Modes

A single page or material is Security Mode or Bartending Mode - never both. Don't put cream sections inside a black page. Each piece commits to one hierarchy.

Amber Text Rule

  • On dark backgrounds: use Amber #C9922E (7.1:1 AAA)
  • On light backgrounds: use Amber Dark #8B6212 (5.4:1 AA)
  • Never use Amber as text on white or cream (FAILS - 2.8:1)

CTA Buttons - Both Modes

  • Amber fill, black text - always
  • The button doesn't change between modes
  • One button style, everywhere - consistent and accessible
06 - Visual Expression
How Events Looks Different

Sheepdog Events shares the parent brand system (logo, Barlow + Source Sans 3, core neutrals) but expresses it with more energy and warmth than Staffing. Amber replaces Signal Red as the primary accent on all Events materials.

Color Emphasis

  • Amber #C9922E is the Events accent - not Signal Red
  • Signal Red #C23B22 reserved for error states only
  • Dual-mode system: Security (dark) ↔ Bartending (cream)
  • Warmer overall feel than Staffing or Hub
  • Never cold/corporate blues as primary tones

Typography Lean

  • H1: Barlow Semi Condensed 800, always uppercase
  • Bold, physical, high-energy display text
  • Short sentences - punchy, direct copy
  • Active voice only - "We show up" not "Security is provided"
  • CTA language: "Get a Quote," "Book Security," "Let's Talk"

Imagery Direction

  • Real on-site photography - candid, never staged
  • Nighttime and low-light is authentic and encouraged
  • Show the work: door management, crowd scenes, bartending in action
  • Energy and atmosphere - the event should feel alive
  • Team in branded gear, visible but not dominating

Layout & Composition

  • More dynamic than Staffing - larger heroes, bolder breaks
  • Full-bleed photography OK for hero sections
  • Dark overlays (60–80% black) for text over images
  • Cards and components follow parent 8px grid
  • Imagery-forward, not data-forward
i

The litmus test: Cover the logo. If the content feels like a party you'd want to attend, it's Events. If it feels like a proposal you'd forward to your VP of Operations, you've drifted into Staffing territory. Events should always feel like someone you'd want at your party - competent, present, approachable.
