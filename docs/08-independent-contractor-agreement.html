Independent Contractor Status

  • Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor (1099), not an employee of Sheepdog.
  • Contractor is responsible for their own federal, state, and local taxes, including self-employment tax.
  • Sheepdog does not withhold taxes, provide benefits, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance for Contractor.
  • Contractor is free to accept or decline any individual assignment without penalty.
  • Contractor controls the manner and method of performing the work, subject to the client's site-specific safety and access requirements.
  • Contractor may work for other companies, including competitors, provided it does not involve disclosing Sheepdog's confidential information.
  • Contractor has no authority to bind Sheepdog to any contract, agreement, or obligation.

Scheduling & Availability

  • Sheepdog will offer assignments as they become available. Contractor is not guaranteed a minimum number of hours.
  • Contractor agrees to confirm or decline assignments within [RESPONSE TIME - e.g. 24 hours] of being offered.
  • Once an assignment is confirmed, Contractor is expected to show up on time and complete the shift. No-shows or last-minute cancellations may result in removal from future scheduling.

Professional Standards

While Contractor controls the manner and method of performing their work, Contractor agrees to the following baseline standards when representing Sheepdog on assignment:

  • Present a professional appearance appropriate to the assignment. Sheepdog may provide branded apparel for consistency, but Contractor is not an employee by virtue of wearing it.
  • No alcohol or drug use before or during assignments.
  • Follow any site-specific safety or access rules set by the client or venue.
  • Treat clients, guests, and fellow contractors with respect.

Pay & Invoicing

  • Contractor will be paid [PAY FREQUENCY - e.g. weekly, bi-weekly] for completed assignments.
  • Pay is based on confirmed hours worked. Disputes must be raised within 48 hours of the shift.
  • Contractor must submit a completed W-9 before receiving any payment.

Confidentiality

Contractor agrees to keep confidential all client information, venue details, event specifics, Sheepdog business operations, and any proprietary information encountered during assignments. This obligation continues after the end of this agreement.

Non-Solicitation of Clients

Contractor acknowledges that through this engagement, they will have access to Sheepdog's client relationships and confidential business information. In exchange for this access, Contractor agrees that during the term of this agreement and for [NON-SOLICIT PERIOD - e.g. 6 months, 12 months] after termination, Contractor will not directly solicit, contact, or contract with any Sheepdog client for services that compete with Sheepdog's offerings, unless Sheepdog provides written consent. This restriction applies only to clients Contractor was introduced to or worked with through Sheepdog.

Termination

  • Either party may end this agreement at any time, for any reason, with or without notice.
  • Sheepdog may immediately terminate if Contractor violates the code of conduct, no-shows, or engages in behavior that reflects poorly on the business.
  • All pay owed for completed work will be paid regardless of reason for termination.

Liability & Indemnification

  • Contractor acknowledges that some assignments involve inherent risks, including but not limited to physical altercations, crowd situations, and working in environments that serve alcohol. Contractor voluntarily assumes these risks.
  • Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Sheepdog from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from Contractor's own actions, negligence, or misconduct while on assignment.
  • Sheepdog agrees to indemnify Contractor from claims arising from Sheepdog's own negligence in assigning Contractor to an unsafe environment that Sheepdog knew or should have known about.
  • Contractor is responsible for maintaining any required certifications (TABC, guard card, etc.) and providing proof upon request.
  • Contractor is responsible for their own transportation to and from assignments and maintains any required vehicle insurance.
