What Sheepdog needs from the Client for setup

  • All materials, decor, signage, and equipment on-site and accessible before the crew arrival time.
  • A Client contact (or designated on-site person) available by phone during setup for questions.
  • Venue access confirmed and unlocked before crew arrival.

Cleanup Scope of Work

If cleanup is included, Sheepdog crew will perform the following:

  • [DESCRIBE CLEANUP TASKS - e.g. trash removal and bagging, sweep/mop hard floors, wipe down tables and surfaces, clean bar area, remove all event debris, return venue to pre-event condition]

What cleanup does NOT include (unless separately agreed)

  • Deep carpet cleaning or stain removal

  • Landscaping, outdoor grounds maintenance, or parking lot cleanup beyond the event footprint

On-Site Changes

  • If the Client requests additional work, crew, or hours on the day of the event, Sheepdog will make reasonable efforts to accommodate.
  • Additional work will be billed at the hourly rate listed in this agreement.
  • Verbal on-site approvals from the Client's designated contact are considered binding for billing purposes.

Third-Party Vendor Coordination

  • Client is responsible for coordinating the schedules of all other vendors (caterers, DJs, florists, photographers, etc.) working the event.
  • Sheepdog crew takes direction from the Client's designated on-site contact only, not from other vendors.
  • If another vendor's delay prevents Sheepdog from starting or completing work on time, the additional time is billable at the hourly rate in this agreement.

Delays & Standby Time

  • If the crew arrives at the scheduled time and cannot begin work (venue not open, another vendor blocking the space, client not available, required materials not on-site), standby time is billed at the full hourly rate from the scheduled arrival time.
  • If the delay exceeds [MAX WAIT TIME - e.g. 1 hour, 2 hours], Sheepdog may reschedule or leave the site. The minimum call time (below) still applies.

Minimum Call Time

Once the crew is dispatched, a minimum of [MINIMUM HOURS - e.g. 2 hours, 4 hours] per crew member will be billed, regardless of how long the actual work takes. This applies even if the job is cancelled after the crew is en route or on-site.

Cancellation Policy

  • 7+ days out: deposit forfeited, no additional charges.
  • Within 7 days: Client owes 50% of estimated total.
  • Within 48 hours: Client owes 100% of estimated total.

Force Majeure

  • Neither party is liable for failure to perform due to events beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to: severe weather, natural disasters, government orders, public health emergencies, venue closures, or civil disturbances.
  • If a force majeure event prevents the event from occurring, the Client's deposit will be applied as a credit toward a future booking within 12 months. If no rebooking occurs within 12 months, the deposit is forfeited.
  • Sheepdog will make reasonable efforts to notify the Client as soon as possible if it cannot fulfill the agreement due to a force majeure event.

Venue Condition & Damage

  • Sheepdog recommends both parties complete a Setup/Takedown Walkthrough Checklist documenting the venue condition before setup begins and after cleanup is complete.
  • Sheepdog is not responsible for pre-existing venue damage, wear, or conditions noted on the walkthrough checklist.
  • If Sheepdog crew damages the venue or Client property during setup, takedown, or cleanup, Sheepdog will notify the Client immediately and is responsible for repair or replacement costs resulting from Sheepdog's negligence.
  • Sheepdog is not responsible for damage caused by event guests, weather, vendor equipment, or conditions outside Sheepdog's control.
  • Client agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Sheepdog from claims related to the event that are not the direct result of Sheepdog's negligence.

Liability & Insurance

  • Sheepdog maintains general liability insurance.
  • Sheepdog crew will exercise reasonable care when handling Client property, decor, and equipment. However, Sheepdog is not liable for damage to fragile, irreplaceable, or high-value items unless the Client has identified them in advance and Sheepdog has agreed in writing to handle them.
  • Client is responsible for removing or securing any high-value personal items, heirlooms, or irreplaceable decor before takedown/cleanup begins, or clearly marking them and communicating handling instructions to the crew lead.
