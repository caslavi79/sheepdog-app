Phase 1
The Seed Round - Reviews 1-10
Goal: 2-3 reviews per week • Timeline: 3-4 weeks

Start with people who already know your work is good. Don't cold-ask anyone yet.

Who to ask first

Make a list of 15-20 people. You only need 10 to say yes. Start here:

  • Past event clients - Text them using the Staff Script. Start with venues you've worked more than once. Send 2-3 texts per week, not all at once.
  • Personal network - Friends and family who have actually seen the team work. Only ask people who can speak to the business honestly. Google detects fake reviews.
  • Friendly venue managers - Bar managers who like working with you. Quick text or ask at the end of your next shift there.

Pacing

Don't rush it. Google flags profiles that get a bunch of reviews overnight.

  • Don't get 10 reviews in 2 days - it looks fake to Google and may trigger a filter
  • Aim for 2-3 per week, spread across different days
  • Mix of review sources (different Google accounts, different devices) happens naturally when you ask real clients

Phase 2
System Mode - Reviews 11-25
Goal: 2-3 reviews per week • Timeline: 5-6 weeks

You have a star rating on Google now. From here on, asking for reviews happens after every single job. No thinking about it, just do it.

Integrate into the post-job workflow

Follow this sequence after every job. Use the Staff Review Script for exact wording.

  • On-site: Team lead asks at wrap-up + hands over review card (every job, no exceptions)
  • Next-day text: Office sends the text script within 24 hours
  • 48-hour email: For corporate/staffing clients, send the email version
  • 3-day nudge: One follow-up text if no review. Then stop.

Physical materials in rotation

Keep review cards and flyers stocked. If you run out, you're not using them enough.

  • Review cards in every team lead's pocket (replenish weekly)
  • Review flyer on the table at vendor check-in areas
  • QR code added to the bottom of invoices and post-event recap emails

Track it

Use the tracker below. After every job, log whether you asked and whether they reviewed. This keeps the team accountable and shows you which channels convert best.

25 Hit 25 reviews - you outrank most local competitors on Google Maps
Phase 3
Ongoing - Maintain Velocity
Goal: 3-5 reviews per month • Timeline: Indefinite

The system is built. Now just keep it running. Don't let it go idle.

Monthly review goal

3-5 new reviews a month. That's about one a week. Google cares about recent reviews more than total reviews. 20 reviews with 3 from this month beats 25 reviews that are all six months old.

Review gap protocol

Two weeks without a new review? Something broke. Check these:

  • Check: are team leads still asking on-site? (If not, retrain.)
  • Check: are next-day texts still going out? (If not, assign someone.)
  • Reach back to recent clients who didn't review - one polite text, no pressure

Seasonal adjustments

Busy months (spring/fall) = more jobs = more review chances. Stack up reviews then. Slow months (winter) = lean on staffing clients and circle back to past clients you haven't asked yet. Don't go dark for three months. Even one or two reviews a month in the off-season keeps you visible.

Ask Cadence Summary

Every completed job follows this sequence. No exceptions, no improvising.

Day 0 (On-Site) Team lead asks in person at wrap-up. Hands over review card.
Day 1 (Text) Send text script between 10am-2pm. Include direct review link.
Day 1-2 (Email) For corporate/staffing clients, send email version instead of or in addition to text.
Day 4 (Nudge) One follow-up text if no review. Then stop. No third ask.

If You Get a Negative Review During the Push

Don't panic. Don't stop asking for reviews.

  • One negative review among 15+ positive ones actually looks more authentic than a perfect 5.0
  • Respond using the Response Templates - stay professional, take it offline
  • Do NOT ask friends/family to flood positive reviews to "bury" it - Google detects this and it can get reviews removed
  • Keep following the system. The best response to one bad review is ten more good ones earned honestly over the next few weeks
  • If the reviewer is willing to talk and you resolve the issue, they may update their review on their own

Respond to Every Single Review

Why this matters

  • Google's algorithm factors in business responsiveness - responding to reviews improves your ranking
  • Potential clients read responses. A thoughtful reply to a 3-star review can convert a prospect better than a 5-star review with no reply.
  • It signals to future reviewers that their review will actually be seen and appreciated - which makes the next person more likely to leave one
  • Use the Response Templates for every reply. Customize the name and event details, but follow the structure.
