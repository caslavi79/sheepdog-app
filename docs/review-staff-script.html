Review Staff Script

Word-for-word scripts for asking clients for Google reviews

1. In-Person (End of Event)

Say this to the client or venue manager at wrap-up. Keep it casual.

Script A - Direct ask

Say this

"Hey, tonight went really well. I appreciate you having us out here. If you get a chance, a quick Google review would mean a lot to us. It helps other venues and planners find us when they're looking for security or bar staff. Takes about 30 seconds." [Hand them the review card or pull up the QR code on your phone.]

Script B - Softer / if they seem busy

Say this

"Hey, I know you're wrapping up so I won't keep you. We really enjoyed working tonight. I'll shoot you a text tomorrow with a link if you want to leave us a quick Google review. No rush at all."

Coaching Notes - In-Person

  • Who asks: The team lead or whoever had the most face time with the client. Not the newest person on the crew.
  • When: At wrap-up, after load-out, before you leave.
  • Have the card ready: Keep 2-3 review cards in your pocket every shift. Hand it over while you're talking.
  • If they say "sure, I'll do it later": Say "I'll text you the link tomorrow just in case." Then actually do it.

2. Text Message (Next Day)

Send the day after the event. Texts convert better than anything else.

Script A - First-time client

Text

Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from Sheepdog. Thanks again for having us out at [Venue/Event] last night. If you've got 30 seconds, a Google review helps us out a ton 👊

[Review Link]

Script B - Repeat client

Text

Hey [Name], always good working with you and the [Venue] crew. If you haven't already, we'd really appreciate a quick Google review. Helps other folks find us when they need reliable event staff.

[Review Link]

Script C - Follow-up nudge (if no review after 3 days)

Text

Hey [Name], no pressure at all. Just wanted to bump this in case it got buried. Here's that review link again if you get a sec: [Review Link]

Coaching Notes - Text

  • Timing: Send between 10am-2pm the next day.
  • Personalize: Use their first name and the venue/event name. No generic blasts.
  • One follow-up max: If they don't review after the nudge, drop it.
  • Include the direct link: The review link, not your website. They should land on the stars immediately.

3. Email (Professional Follow-Up)

For corporate clients, staffing accounts, or when text feels too casual.

Script A - Events client

Subject line

Thanks for a great night at [Venue/Event Name]

Body

Hi [Name],

Thanks again for having us out at [Venue/Event]. The team had a great time working it, and we're glad everything ran smoothly on our end.

If you have 30 seconds, a quick Google review goes a long way for a local business like ours. Here's the direct link:

[Review Link]

Appreciate it, and hope to work with you again soon.

[Your Name]
The Sheepdog Team

Script B - Staffing / corporate client

Subject line

Following up on your [Company Name] placement

Body

Hi [Name],

Wanted to follow up on the recent [placement/project type] with [Company Name]. Our team delivered on scope and on schedule, and we appreciate the opportunity to support your operations.

If you have a moment, a Google review from partners like [Company Name] helps other operations managers find reliable staffing when they need it. Here's the direct link:

[Review Link]

Thanks for the continued partnership.

[Your Name]
Sheepdog Staffing

Coaching Notes - Email

  • Timing: Send within 48 hours. Sooner is better.
  • From who: Send from the person they actually worked with, not a generic inbox.
  • Reply-to: Use [email protected] so replies go to the team inbox.
  • Don't attach anything. No invoices, no PDFs. Keep it single-purpose.

General Rules

Do

  • Ask after every completed job - make it automatic
  • Reference the specific event, venue, or company by name
  • Hand them the physical review card on-site
  • Follow up by text the next day (highest conversion)
  • Keep it to one ask + one nudge max
  • Alternate between text and email based on the client relationship

Don't

  • Ask during the event (wait until wrap-up or after)
  • Send the same copy-paste message to everyone
  • Ask more than twice - if they don't do it, move on
  • Offer incentives for reviews (violates Google policy)
  • Ask in the same email as an invoice or contract
  • Have the newest crew member ask - send your most personable lead
