Permits & Licensing

  • Client is responsible for obtaining any event-specific alcohol permits required by Texas law or the venue.
  • Sheepdog does not hold a liquor license and does not sell alcohol. Sheepdog provides bartending labor only.
  • If the event requires a temporary alcohol permit, the Client must have it on-site before bar service begins.

Bar Equipment & Cleanup

  • If Sheepdog provides bar equipment, Client is responsible for damage or loss not caused by Sheepdog staff.
  • Sheepdog bartenders will clean and break down the bar area. General venue cleanup is the Client's responsibility.

Force Majeure

  • Neither party is liable for failure to perform due to events beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to: severe weather, natural disasters, government orders, public health emergencies, venue closures, or civil disturbances.
  • If a force majeure event prevents the event from occurring, the Client's deposit will be applied as a credit toward a future booking within 12 months. If no rebooking occurs within 12 months, the deposit is forfeited.
  • Sheepdog will make reasonable efforts to notify the Client as soon as possible if it cannot fulfill the agreement due to a force majeure event.

On-Site Changes

  • If the Client requests additional staff, extended hours, or expanded scope on the day of the event, Sheepdog will make reasonable efforts to accommodate.
  • Additional hours or staff will be billed at the rates listed in this agreement.
  • Verbal on-site approvals from the Client's designated contact are considered binding for billing purposes.
